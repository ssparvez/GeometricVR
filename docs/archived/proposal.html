Objective

To create a mobile VR app that provides a new dimension to learning 3D geometry. There will be a grid of several three dimensional shapes that the user can choose from the menu screen. When a shape is chosen, the user will be 3D representation of that shape with properties such as volume and surface area listed at the top of the view. The formulas for these properties will also be provided. The user can manipulate this object by zooming and rotating through the use of the VR headset trigger. Arrows on the left and right side will allow the user to switch between the next and previous shapes. There will also be extra information displayed at the bottom of the view about the specified 3D object. In addition, there will be an option to take a simple test to see if you know your shapes.

Specifications

Use of this application requires a smartphone running at least Android 6/iOS 8 or newer. A VR headset such as Google Cardboard is also required. The application does not require constant internet connection.

Target Audience

This application is intended mainly for students that are currently learning geometry. The primary goal of the application is help students learn geometry anywhere, whether it's at home or at school.

School systems are encouraged to trial our application with students for field testing purposes.

Similar Products

There are many sites that can teach basic math (such as www.khanacademy.org or www.mathisfun.com), however none of these resources are truly fun or engaging and many look very outdated in the modern technological world. Our project is intended engage children in learning Math by creating a competitive and fun environment. The VR feature seperates our project from the others by offering a unique interactive dimension.
